Espresso

An espresso is a concentrated form of coffee served in small, strong shots, and it is the base for many coffee drinks, such as latte, cappuccino, macchiato.

Ingredients

  • Finely ground coffee

Instructions

  1. Grind the coffee beans with a grinder. An average single shot of espresso will require 6 to 8 grams of coffee and a double shot will require approximately 15 grams). The espresso ground should be powdery and fine, so use the finest setting on your grinder. Place the portafilter on the scale and add coffee to the desired weight.

  2. Place the filled portafilter to a counter or a flate surface. Gently distribute the grounds evenly with a finger, then use a tamper to tamp down the grounds to create a compact disk of espresso in the portafilter.

  3. Purge the machine by running it briefly without a portafilter in place to clear the ground head. Then, lock the portafilter into the machine, position a glass that can hold around 1/2- 1dl (2- to 3 ounce), or something else underneath, and pull your shot. The ideal brewing time for an espresso is between 20 to 30 seconds. The espresso should have a fine layer of caramel-colored crema on top.

  4. If you want to make a cappuccino or other drink with milk, you will then have to steam the milk.
